üåûVowel-Consonant Strings

Yes, this seems not look hard, because you know whether any letter is a vowel or consonant.

But there are two special rules:

  1. W can become a vowel at the end of a morphograph.
  2. Y can become a vowel too at the end of a morphograph.
Try it out!

üåø Morphograph combining

You may say that to combine two morphographs are just putting them together.

For example...

  • play + ing = playing
  • play + ful = playful
  • care + ed = careed?

NO!!!

There are some more rules to combine them!!!

Look carefully...

  • Drop the final 'e' when followed by a vowel morphograph...
  • Double the final consonant for short CVC words followed by a vowel...
  • Change y to i when preceded by a consonant and not followed by 'i'...
  • If they do not apply, just put them together.
Try it out!

üåå Pluralization

Do you think you can pluralize a word by just adding s?
Well, it's not always that simple! There are exceptions to consider.

Here are some rules:

  • Add 'es' to words ending in s, x, z, or o...
  • also add 'es' to words ending in 'ch' or 'sh'...
  • consonant + y becomes 'ies'...
  • 'fe' becomes 'ves'...
  • and 'f' becomes 'ves'.
  • For most other words, you can just add 's'.
  • Remember to check for irregular plurals!
Try it out!
